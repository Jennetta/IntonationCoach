The most common intonation pattern in French is falling intonation, in which the pitch is lowest at the end of the sentence. Falling intonation is found in most declarative sentences (statements), as well as imperative sentences (commands). For example:

Declarative: “Hier, je suis allé au supermarché.”


Imperative: “Tournez à la page cent-vingt-deux.”

French has different intonation patterns for various types of questions. A basic yes-or-no question has rising intonation, meaning the pitch is the highest at the end of the sentence:

Yes/No Interrogative: “Est-ce que vous partez en vacances aujourd'hui?”

In contrast, a question that asks for information (instead of a yes or no) tends to have falling intonation at the end.

Information Interrogative: “Comment vous appelez-vous?”

Exclamations in French follow a pattern of starting at a high pitch and falling sharply. Depending on the length of the sentence, there are often multiple peaks towards the beginning.

Exclamatory: “Quel beau temps on a eu récemment!”
